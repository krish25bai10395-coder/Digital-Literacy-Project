

Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud Among College Students : 

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which scammers trick users into transferring money through fake links, QR codes, or fraudulent calls. In many cases, students receive a phone call from someone pretending to be a bank official or customer care executive. The scammer claims there is an issue with the victim’s bank account or offers a cashback reward.
The fraud usually happens step-by-step. First, the scammer builds trust by sounding professional and urgent. Second, they send a fake payment request or QR code through WhatsApp or SMS. Third, they convince the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is needed to “receive money.” However, entering the UPI PIN actually authorizes a payment, and money is transferred from the victim’s account.
College students are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps and may not be fully aware of security practices. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and difficulty recovering funds. In serious cases, victims may lose their entire bank balance. This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and cautious online financial behavior.
